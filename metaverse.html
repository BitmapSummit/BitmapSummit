MECHANICS

How It Works

The Bitmap Metaverse is built on a simple but profound idea: Bitcoin block data can be mapped to spatial analogues. Every piece of information inside a Bitcoin block — transactions, values, sizes, timestamps — can be interpreted as properties of virtual land.

To claim a District, you must be the first person to inscribe {block_height}.bitmap onto a satoshi. This inscription is verified by indexers which scan the Bitcoin blockchain for valid Bitmap claims.

Once claimed, the block's data is parsed by indexers and rendered into a 3D or 2D spatial visualization — creating a unique piece of land that no one else can replicate, because no two Bitcoin blocks contain identical data.

// Data Mapping Logic

block.value → Land Area
// Higher value = larger district

block.vbytes → Land Depth
// More data = deeper land in 3D

block.tx_count → Parcel Count
// More transactions = more parcels

tx.input_size → Parcel Size
// Larger inputs = larger parcels

block.timestamp → Historical Context
// When the block was mined

✓ Verifiable on-chain

The District-Parcel System

🏙️
District (Bitcoin Block)
A District is one entire Bitcoin block. It is the primary unit of ownership in the Bitmap metaverse. The block height number (e.g. 431897) is the District ID. Owning the District means owning all Parcels within it.
🏘️
Parcel (Transaction)
Each transaction in the block is a Parcel — a subdivision of land. Parcels inherit from their parent District. When the District is sold, all Parcels move with it. Each block can have hundreds or even thousands of Parcels.
🎨
Blocktribute (Build)
Only verified District owners can add Blocktributes — custom attributes, art, or metadata inscribed to their land. Verified using Bitcoin's UTXO set: the inscription must be sent to the same address as the District.
🗺️
Canvas (Visualization)
The District canvas is a 2D or 3D rendering of the block's transaction data. Each Parcel appears as a colored region. The visual is generated algorithmically from real block data — unique to each block forever.
THE CONCEPT

Digital Matter Theory

Bitmap is grounded in Digital Matter Theory (DMT) — a philosophical framework that treats on-chain data as having intrinsic, matter-like properties. Just as physical matter has shape, mass, and structure, digital data has size, value, and sequence.

Under DMT, a Bitcoin block is not just a database entry — it's a distinct digital substance. Its properties are fixed, observable, and real. Bitmap maps these properties to the spatial metaphor humans use for land: area, depth, and location.

This is why Bitmaps are called "non-arbitrary" — their nature is determined by the physical reality of Bitcoin computation, not the whims of a designer.

"Bitmap Theory represents the mapping of Bitcoin's data landscape to spatial analogues, bringing to light the base substrate of a persistent Metaverse." — Bitmap Gitbook
Unlike NFTs with randomized traits or arbitrary art, every property of a Bitmap is derived from real, immutable, verifiable Bitcoin blockchain data. The blockchain is the artist.
FOUNDATION

Purpose, Vision, Mission & Goal

🎯
PURPOSE

To establish a decentralized, fair, and permanent consensus layer for metaverse land ownership on the Bitcoin blockchain — where the only cost is energy and the rules are enforced by Bitcoin's proof-of-work, not corporations.

🌐
VISION

A persistent, open metaverse where every layer of reality is anchored to Bitcoin. Not one metaverse — but an infinite foundation for many metaverses. Bitcoin as the immutable substrate of the digital world. Any team can build a client. Any indexer can render Bitmaps. No single company owns the standard.

⚙️
MISSION

To provide an equitable and decentralized process for transmuting Bitcoin blocks into metaverse districts — accessible to anyone, controlled by no one, permanent forever. To prove that Bitcoin can be the base layer for human digital experience, not just digital money.

🏆
GOAL

To become the universal land standard on Bitcoin that supports: 3D metaverse environments, gaming, social spaces, creator economies, DeFi integration, and cross-chain interoperability — all rooted in the immutability of the Bitcoin blockchain.

🔬
LOGIC

Bitcoin blocks are produced forever. Each is unique. Their data is permanent. Mapping this data to spatial ownership creates an asset class that is inherently scarce per block, infinitely expandable, and backed by the world's most secure database. The logic is elegant and irrefutable.

💡
CONCEPT SUMMARY

Bitcoin is the world's most immutable ledger. Every block is a permanent record. Bitmap proposes that these records are not just financial entries — they are pieces of digital territory. Own a block. Own the land. Build on it. Forever.

ECOSYSTEM PROTOCOL

BRC-420 — The Asset Layer

Built on top of Bitmap by the RCSV/Recursiverse team, BRC-420 is the asset management protocol that brings the metaverse to life.

BRC-420 allows multiple Ordinals inscriptions to be recursively combined into complex inscriptions — enabling game characters, animations, scripts, music, and even AI models to be inscribed entirely on-chain.

These composable modules become playable in the Bitmap metaverse. Bitmap is the land; BRC-420 is everything built on it — furniture, avatars, games, and economies.

Over 100 teams have issued assets on BRC-420, generating $10M+ in cumulative royalties. The protocol powers Bitmap Game, Bitmap War, Mineral, and dozens of other projects.
